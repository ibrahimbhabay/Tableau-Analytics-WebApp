Climate Change

Climate change is most certainly a fact of life. With the weather patterns getting tempestuous, melting of glaciers and the rising sea levels, millions of people around the world are at risk. IPCC, an inter-governmental body of United Nations has acertained that the warming of climate systems is unambiguous. Thirty-four distinct national academies of science have agreed that the climate change is manmade.

Professor David Griggs, a climate scientist and atmospheric physicist, he works at Monash sustainable development institute at Monash University. In his interview with ABC new in June 2017, he said, “Australia is the most vulnerable developed countries in the world to climate change.”

In the past couple of years, regional areas in Queensland and Northern Australia have been experiencing increasing number of droughts, due to rise in temperature.
